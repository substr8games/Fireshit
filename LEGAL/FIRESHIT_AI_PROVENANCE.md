# Fireshit AI Provenance Policy
## Version 0.1

## 0. Authority

AI systems are tools. They are not authors, copyright owners, licensors, maintainers, or contributors to Fireshit.

No raw model output enters the canonical repository automatically.

## 1. Human acceptance gate

Before AI-assisted material becomes first-party Fireshit material, an authorized human contributor must:

1. review it in full;
2. understand its purpose and operation;
3. verify technical and legal provenance to the extent reasonably possible;
4. make necessary design and implementation decisions;
5. integrate it into Fireshit architecture;
6. test or otherwise validate it;
7. remove suspicious third-party material;
8. accept responsibility for the final contribution; and
9. create the repository record under the human contributor’s identity.

## 2. Prohibited provenance shortcuts

The following are not sufficient:

- “the model wrote it”;
- lack of a visible license header;
- the model’s claim that output is original;
- paraphrasing a named source while preserving its expression;
- changing variable names in copied code;
- removing copyright or attribution comments; or
- assuming generated graphics are free of recognizable protected assets.

## 3. Third-party material

Generated output that reproduces or substantially adapts third-party material remains third-party material.

It must be:

- admitted under the Fireshit Third-Party Material Policy;
- rewritten from requirements without copying protected expression; or
- rejected.

## 4. Recordkeeping

For substantial AI-assisted contributions, retain a concise provenance record containing:

- date;
- human contributor;
- affected files;
- tool or model category, when known;
- purpose of assistance;
- named source material supplied to the tool;
- third-party concerns found;
- human modifications made;
- tests or validation performed; and
- acceptance decision.

Prompts need not be published unless project policy separately requires publication.

## 5. Public authorship

Repository metadata must identify the responsible human contributor.

A model may be acknowledged as a tool in development notes, but must not appear as:

- copyright holder;
- legal author;
- contributor signatory;
- licensor; or
- owner.

## 6. Registration and claims

Any copyright filing or enforcement statement must accurately distinguish human-authored material from material known to be purely machine-generated.

Fireshit claims human authorship in the selection, arrangement, modification, integration, architecture, design decisions, and original expression actually supplied by people.
