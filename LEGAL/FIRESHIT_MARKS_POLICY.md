## 1. Controlled identities

Controlled Fireshit identities include:

- Fireshit
- Firemod
- FrameTap
- SUBSTR8 HUD
- Firestate
- the canonical Fireshit suite icon
- official module icons
- official compatibility badges
- official graphical lockups and distinctive project identity assets

## 2. Uses that do not require permission

You may:

- refer to Fireshit truthfully;
- discuss, review, criticize, document, or report on Fireshit;
- display screenshots showing Fireshit in operation;
- link to an official release;
- state that an Independent Work is compatible with Fireshit when that statement is accurate;
- state that a fork is based on a specified Fireshit version; and
- reproduce unmodified marks as they appear inside an exact authorized copy of Fireshit.

Examples:

> Compatible with Fireshit.

> Based on Fireshit version [VERSION].

> Requires FrameTap.

These permissions do not authorize a claim of ownership, sponsorship, certification, endorsement, or official status.

## 3. Uses requiring written permission

Written permission from Giz@SUBSTR8Games is required to:

- use a controlled mark in the primary name of a fork, product, service, package repository, domain, or organization;
- use a controlled mark as the dominant part of a product logo;
- modify, recolor, remix, animate, or repurpose official brand assets;
- produce merchandise using controlled marks;
- use a mark in commercial advertising for an unrelated product;
- distribute a Modified Version under a controlled Fireshit name; or
- claim official, certified, approved, endorsed, supported, or verified status.

## 4. Forks and modified distributions

A fork or Modified Version must use a distinct primary name and visual identity.

A fork may truthfully state:

> Based on Fireshit version [VERSION].

A fork may not present itself as an official continuation, authorized release, replacement project, or canonical Fireshit distribution unless written permission says otherwise.

Official icons and logos may not be used as the fork’s primary identity.

## 5. Compatibility claims

Independent projects may use factual compatibility language.

Compatibility language must not be visually or verbally presented as certification.

The following terms are reserved unless granted in writing:

- Official Fireshit
- Fireshit Certified

- Fireshit Verified
- Supported by Fireshit
- Endorsed by SUBSTR8 Games

The Fireshit project may publish and revise its own compatibility indexes, technical ratings, warnings, and support classifications. Inclusion in or removal from those indexes does not change the independent project’s software license.

Brand assets are licensed only for the limited uses stated in this policy and FCIL.

They may not be extracted from the suite and reused as stock assets, interface decorations, product icons, mascots, merchandise art, or branding for unrelated works.

An exact unmodified Fireshit distribution may include the original assets required to operate and identify that distribution.
